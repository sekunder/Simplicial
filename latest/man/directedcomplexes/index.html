Directed Complexes

A directed complex $(V, D)$ is a finite set $V$, called the vertex set, together with a collection $D$ of "proper sequences" on $V$, closed under taking subsequences. A "proper sequence" is a a sequence $(v_1,v_2,\dots,v_k)$ with $v_i \in V$ and no $v_i$s repeated. The closure property says that if $s = (v_1,\dots,v_k) \in D$, then every subsequence of $s$ is also in $D$.
